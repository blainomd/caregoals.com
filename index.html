Why nobody has this conversation

Nobody wants to have this conversation. It feels like inviting the topic of death in. There's a sense that bringing it up means giving up — that talking about the end signals you've stopped fighting for more time.

But families who have it describe the experience as a relief, not a burden. When the hard questions are answered before a crisis, something shifts. There's less fear. Less guessing. Less arguing in hospital hallways about what Mom would have wanted.

CareGoals is built to make this conversation feel like what it actually is: a gift from one generation to another.

How CareGoals works

Four steps from first question to shared care summary.

chat
Step 1

Sage asks gentle questions

About values, preferences, what matters most — not just yes/no medical decisions.

description
Step 2

Answers become a care summary

Organized, readable, and ready to share — not buried in a legal document.

group
Step 3

Share with family and doctors

Designate who can see it. Share directly with your care team when the time comes.

update
Step 4

Update anytime

Circumstances change. Sage remembers your history and builds on each new conversation.

What gets covered

Six conversation areas, each handled with care and clarity.

medical_services

Medical preferences

CPR, hospitalization, life support, comfort-focused care versus aggressive treatment.

home

Living arrangements

Home care, assisted living, nursing facilities — and what daily life should feel like.

account_balance

Financial decisions

Who handles finances if you can't, and what financial priorities matter most to you.

person_check

Who speaks for you

Healthcare proxy, durable power of attorney, who you trust to carry out your wishes.

sunny

Daily life preferences

Routines, privacy, food, visitors, what a good day looks like when things are hard.

auto_stories

Legacy and meaning

What you want people to remember, the stories that define you, the values you want to pass on.
