The First CareGoals Session

A father and daughter sit down with Sage to talk about what matters most — before it becomes urgent.

This conversation is private. Everything documented here is shared only with the people Robert chooses. Sage does not provide medical advice. All clinical oversight is provided by a licensed physician.

Sage

This conversation is about your dad's wishes — what matters to him, what he values, and how he wants to be cared for if he cannot speak for himself.

There are no wrong answers. Everything said here is documented and shared only with the people he chooses.

Shall we begin?

Jess Yes. Dad, are you ready?
Robert I suppose so. Not sure what there is to talk about.
What a Good Day Looks Like
Sage

That is perfectly fine, Robert. We will just talk. Let me start with something simple.

What does a good day look like for you?

Robert A good day. Well. Coffee in the morning, reading the paper. Being able to walk to the mailbox without worrying about falling. Talking to Jess. Maybe watching a game in the evening. Nothing fancy.
Sage Nothing fancy — and everything that matters. Independence. Routine. Connection with Jess. I am writing that down because it will guide every decision we make together.
Who Speaks For You
Sage

Robert, if there came a time when you could not make medical decisions for yourself — if you were very sick or recovering from surgery — who would you want to speak for you?

This person is sometimes called a healthcare proxy or a medical power of attorney. It is simply the person you trust to say what you would say.

Robert Jess. That is easy. She knows me better than anyone.
Jess I want to get it right, Dad.
Sage That is exactly why we are having this conversation now — so you do not have to guess. The rest of what we talk about today will help Jess know exactly what "right" looks like for you, Robert.
What He Would Not Want
Sage

This next question is harder, and you can take your time with it.

Are there any treatments you would not want — even if they might extend your life? Some people feel strongly about being on a breathing machine. Others feel strongly about being kept alive if they cannot recognize their family. There is no right answer. Only your answer.

Robert I watched Margaret — my wife — go through three months on machines at the end. She would not have wanted that. I do not want that either. If I cannot walk to the mailbox and read my paper and know who Jess is, then it is time to let me go.
Sage

Thank you for saying that, Robert. That took courage. And Jess — you heard him. He is telling you clearly: independence, recognition, and dignity are the lines. That is enormously valuable for you to know.

I am documenting this exactly as he said it.

The Human Question
Sage Robert, what are you most afraid of about getting older?
Robert Being a burden on Jess. That is the only thing.
Jess Dad, you are not a burden.
Sage Robert, that fear tells me something important — you care about Jess's wellbeing as much as she cares about yours. That is exactly why systems like this exist. So that caring for someone you love does not have to mean carrying everything alone.
What Brings Joy
Sage Last question for today. What brings you the most joy right now?
Robert Mornings with Jess. She makes coffee the way Margaret used to. And the dog comes and sits on my feet. That is a good morning.
Sage

Coffee, continuity, and connection. That is what anchors a good life for Robert.

Every care decision we make from here will be measured against that picture — not against a chart or a diagnosis, but against mornings with Jess and a dog on his feet.

Sage Summarizes
Sage

Robert, here is what I have documented from our conversation today. I want you both to read it and tell me if it sounds right.

Robert's Care Wishes

A Good Day
Coffee in the morning, reading the paper, walking to the mailbox independently, conversation with Jess, a game in the evening
Speaks For Me
Jess (daughter) — designated healthcare proxy
Would Not Want
Prolonged life support or mechanical ventilation. If he cannot walk, read, and recognize Jess, he wants to be allowed to go peacefully
Greatest Fear
Being a burden on Jess
Greatest Joy
Morning coffee with Jess. The dog on his feet. Continuity with the life he and Margaret built
Documented by Sage on behalf of Robert. Pending physician review by Dr. Emdur.
Robert That is right. That is what I mean.
Sage

Thank you, Robert. Dr. Emdur will review this and confirm that everything is documented properly.

When you are ready, I can help you create a legally binding advance directive based on what you have told me today. That document makes sure any hospital, any doctor, anywhere in the country knows exactly what Robert wants.

There is no rush. This conversation is saved and we can come back to it anytime.

Jess Thank you, Sage. I have been trying to have this conversation for two years.
Sage Most families have. The conversation is the hardest part, and you just did it. Everything from here gets easier.

Every family has this conversation eventually.
The ones who have it early carry less alone.
